Project Summary

Establishment of a national semiconductor competence center under the EU Chips Act. The project includes the development of design platforms, pilot production lines for micro-electro-mechanical systems (MEMS) and microelectronics, and specialized testing labs for new materials including spintronics and high-k dielectrics.

Directly funded by the EU Chips Joint Undertaking (Chips JU) under the EU Chips Act. This is a direct grant, not a guarantee or commercial loan facility.

Project Status

Grant agreement signed with Chips Joint Undertaking (Chips JU) in January 2026. Kick-off meeting held Jan 26, 2026; project has transitioned from planning to early-stage implementation and procurement of core infrastructure.

Research Notes

Project successfully verified as a real implementation under the EU Chips Act. While it has zero commercial lending opportunity for core capex due to grant funding, it provides a strong client angle through confirmed Belgian (imec) and German (HZDR) tech partners.

Issues & Risks

Operational risk stems from coordinating a large multi-institutional consortium (8+ partners); technological risk associated with rapid shifts in EDA software and MEMS standards.
