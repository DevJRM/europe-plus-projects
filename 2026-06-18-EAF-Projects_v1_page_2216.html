Project Summary

Strategic 58km motorway section through the Carpathian Mountains (Prahova Valley), part of the TEN-T Core Network. The project aims to eliminate a critical transport bottleneck between Bucharest and Transylvania. Currently stalled in pre-tendering due to significant design and geotechnical study deficiencies identified in early 2026.

Sponsor

CNIR (Compania Nationala de Investitii Rutiere)

  • Government of Romania (RO)
Guarantor

sovereign

Sovereign backed via Romanian state budget; however, the 2026 national budget allocated zero funds for construction, creating a total reliance on future IFI (EIB/EBRD) or EU tranches.

Project Scope

Carpathian Tunnels €825.0m (55.0%)
Construction of complex mountain tunnels totaling approximately 10km, including the Tarluta pass segment.
Viaducts and Earthworks €450.0m (30.0%)
Major bridge structures and massive excavation required by mountainous terrain.
Roadway and Systems €225.0m (15.0%)
Pavement, signage, and Intelligent Transport Systems (ITS).

Financial Structure

Financing Status

not yet secured

UNFUNDED: Main construction capex is unsecured. EIB signed a EUR 200m RRF co-financing agreement in 2023 for broader A3 preparation, which does not cover the Comarnic-Brașov main works. Total financing gap estimated at EUR 1.5B.

ECA/MLA Involvement

eligible

Eligible for EIB/EBRD support. Direct IFI lending is expected to leave a commercial gap; participation of IT/AT/FR firms would enable SACE, OeKB, or Bpifrance ECA tranches.

Project Status

Stalled in pre-tendering phase. In March 2026, the designer (Consitrans) was fined approx. EUR 800k by CNIR for geotechnical deficiencies (missing borings), risking contract termination and delaying the main tender launch to 2027.

Research Notes

Project updated as of April 08, 2026. Capex adjusted from 3.8B to 1.5B to reflect the specific 58km section costs (removing conflation with the full corridor). Sponsor updated to CNIR to reflect 2025/2026 restructuring.

Issues & Risks

Long history of failed procurement; high complexity/cost due to mountain terrain; severe geotechnical data gaps identified in March 2026 delaying tender readiness.

Updates

2026-03: CNIR penalized designer Consitrans with approx. EUR 800k fine for failure to complete mandatory geotechnical drillings.
