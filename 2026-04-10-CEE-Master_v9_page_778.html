Project Summary

Baltica 3 is a 1,036 MW offshore wind farm located in the Polish Baltic Sea, approximately 22 km from the shore near Łeba and Ustka. Jointly developed by PGE (50%) and Ørsted (50%), it is a flagship project in Poland's offshore wind program. The project secured a 25-year Contract for Difference (CfD) from the Polish Energy Regulatory Office (URE). While originally targeting 2027, the Commercial Operation Date (COD) is currently anticipated by 2030 due to industry-wide supply chain and vessel availability constraints.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

not yet secured

Anticipated non-recourse project finance structure of ~EUR 4B. While the EIB signed a EUR 1.003B facility in May 2025 for the combined Baltica II & III portfolio, the primary commercial debt syndication for Baltica 3 specific capex remains open and is targetted for closure closer to the 2027 FID.

ECA/MLA Involvement

eligible

EIB signed a EUR 1.003B facility for the Baltica portfolio in May 2025. EKF (Denmark) and KUKE (Poland) are highly likely to provide guarantees for commercial tranches linked to Danish turbine content and Polish local content respectively.

Project Status

The project is in advanced planning/pre-construction. While Baltica 2 (sister project) reached FID in early 2025, Baltica 3 is progressing toward a separate FID expected by 2027. Preliminary site works and O&M base construction in Ustka are underway. Offshore construction of the main farm has not yet commenced.

Research Notes

Project Baltica 3 is distinct from Bałtyk 3 (Equinor/Polenergia). Corrected status from construction to planning based on updated 2026 data showing FID for Phase 3 is distinct from Phase 2.

Issues & Risks

Key execution risks include: (1) Cost inflation in steel and turbine components; (2) Vessel scarcity for offshore installation campaigns; (3) Grid synchronization with Polish TSO (PSE); (4) Currency mismatch between PLN-denominated CfD and EUR-denominated capex.

Updates

2026-02: Polish Energy Regulator issued concession promise updates for the Baltica portfolio in Feb 2026. Construction of the O&M base in Ustka is progressing.
