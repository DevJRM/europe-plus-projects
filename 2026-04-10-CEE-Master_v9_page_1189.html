Project Summary

A strategic investment cycle for the Impol Seval aluminum mill in Sevojno, focusing on the modernization of cold rolling and finishing lines. The goal is to shift production toward high-purity foils and automotive-grade aluminum sheets to meet EU supply chain requirements.

Sponsor

Impol Seval a.d.

Guarantor

corporate

The facility is expected to be backed by a corporate guarantee from the parent entity, Impol d.o.o. (Slovenia).

Contracted Suppliers

No suppliers identified

Potential Supply Opportunities

SMS Group (DE)
SMS Group is a historical supplier of rolling mill equipment to Impol Seval and maintains a service presence in the region. Reported as a lead candidate for the 2027 automation package.
Primetals Technologies (AT)
Frequent bidder for aluminum mill modernizations in Central and Eastern Europe; known to have bid on previous Impol Group upgrades.

Project Scope

Cold Rolling Mill Upgrade €38.5m (70.0%)
Integration of advanced automation and precision thickness control systems.
Finishing Line Expansion €16.5m (30.0%)
Installation of high-speed slitting and specialized coating equipment for automotive components.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

not yet secured

While 40% is earmarked from internal cash flow, the 60% external debt component (approx. EUR 33M) is currently being negotiated with commercial lenders for sub-allocation in 2026.

ECA/MLA Involvement

eligible

Given the high proportion of imported equipment from Germany/Austria, the project is eligible for ECA cover (Euler Hermes/OeKB). No formal applications confirmed as of April 2026.

Project Status

The investment plan was formally approved by the board of the Slovenian parent company, Impol Group, in late 2025. Procurement is expected to commence in H2 2026 for a 2027 start.

Research Notes

Project is essential for the sponsor to maintain its status as a Tier-2 supplier for European OEMs. Financing is anticipated to close by Q4 2026.

Issues & Risks

Exposure to fluctuations in LME aluminum prices and energy costs in the Serbian market.
