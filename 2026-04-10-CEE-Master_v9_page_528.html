Project WISŁA Phase II - Patriot System Expansion

Expansion to existing and new air defense sites including Sochaczew, Warsaw, and sites in Eastern Poland for NATO flank support.

Project Summary

The second phase of Poland's medium-range air and missile defense program, involving the acquisition of 6 additional Patriot batteries (fire units) reaching a total of 8, equipped with 12 Lower Tier Air and Missile Defense Sensors (LTAMDS) 360-degree GhostEye radars and an undisclosed number of PAC-3 MSE missiles.

Sponsor

Directly backed by the Polish State Treasury. Supported by US Foreign Military Financing (FMF) loan guarantees administered by the DSCA.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

partially secured

A $4bn FMF loan guarantee tranche was signed in July 2025. Significant commercial financing is anticipated for the Polish industrial tranches (PGZ consortium) to handle local production/offsets and infrastructure estimated at ~40% of phase value.

ECA/MLA Involvement

confirmed

US Foreign Military Financing (FMF) confirmed; $4bn loan guarantee signed July 25, 2025. This functions as a credit enhancement for sovereign borrowing.

Project Status

Phase II is in advanced procurement; implementation of LOA (Letter of Acceptance) signed in late 2023. Deliveries are scheduled to begin in 2027 and run through 2029.

Research Notes

Project WISŁA Phase II represents one of the largest defense acquisitions in European history. While government-to-government (FMS) agreements cover the US-made hardware, the 'industrial gap' for Polish PGZ partners remains the primary commercial banking opportunity. Total Capex is a market estimate based on the $15b DSCA notification ceiling vs actual contracted tranches.

Issues & Risks

Integration complexity involving the new 360-degree LTAMDS radar with the IBCS architecture; supply chain pressure on PAC-3 MSE missile production volumes; political sensitivity regarding potential diversion of batteries to other conflict zones.

Updates

2026-03-31: Poland officially rejected requests to divert Patriot batteries to the Middle East, confirming Phase II expansion remains the absolute national defense priority with a firm 2027 arrival target.
