Project Summary

The ZTPOK waste-to-energy plant in Bydgoszcz, operational since 2016 with an annual capacity of approximately 180,000 tonnes, is the subject of high-level conceptual discussions regarding a potential Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS) or BECCS retrofit. As of April 2026, no formal investment decision, feasibility study, or site-specific procurement for a CCS unit has been announced by the municipal operator.

Project Status

The CCS modernization is at a pre-conceptual/monitoring stage. Local municipal priority in 2026 has shifted to the construction of a new biogas plant (PLN 129M), with no budget allocated for CCS at ZTPOK in the current investment cycle.

Research Notes

Project remains [SUSPICIOUS]. Extensive verification confirms the base plant is real, but the CCS modernization lacks any primary documentation (tender, FEED, or council resolution). Municipal budget is currently dedicated to a biogas facility.

Issues & Risks

High energy penalty (parasitic load) reducing net power output; absence of CO2 transport and storage infrastructure in the Kuyavia-Pomerania region; lack of official municipal backing.

Updates

2026-03: Bydgoszcz officially commenced construction of a PLN 129M biogas plant in March 2026, which is currently the city's primary environmental capex priority.
