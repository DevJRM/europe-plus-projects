Project Summary

Construction of two new nuclear reactors (Units 7 and 8) at the existing Kozloduy NPP site using Westinghouse AP1000 technology. The project aims to add 2.4GW of capacity to replace aging thermal and nuclear units, ensuring long-term energy security. Target operational dates are 2035 for Unit 7 and 2037 for Unit 8.

Project Status

FEED/Engineering Services Contract (ESC) ongoing since Nov 2024 with Westinghouse-Hyundai consortium. Owner's Engineer contract with BWXT-led consortium signed Dec 2025. Final Investment Decision (FID) and EPC award targeted for late 2026/2027.

Research Notes

Estimated capex adjusted to EUR 14.5bn based on March 2026 economic benefit projections. Structure confirmed as SOE-led with high probability of sovereign-backed ECA guarantee tranches for commercial lenders.

Issues & Risks

Integration of US technology and South Korean EPC under Bulgarian/EU regulatory framework; secure nuclear fuel supply diversification away from Russian sources; long-term sovereign debt capacity management for EUR 14bn+ investment.

Updates

2026-03: Bulgarian government socialized economic impact study projecting EUR 55bn GDP benefit; preparing EU state aid notification for the financing structure.
