Project Summary

Construction of a new 60,000 sqm state-of-the-art children's hospital in Belgrade to address pediatric capacity shortages and replace the outdated Tiršova facility. It features specialized wards, advanced diagnostics, and emergency units as a regional excellence center under the EU Global Gateway initiative.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

secured

Total project cost of €141M is fully secured: €104M via two CEB loans (€54M initial + €50M additional approved Oct 2025), a €35.7M EU WBIF grant, and the remainder from the state budget. No financing gap for commercial lenders.

ECA/MLA Involvement

confirmed

Council of Europe Development Bank (CEB) and Western Balkans Investment Framework (WBIF) are direct lenders/grantors. Their direct participation covers the full project cost, crowding out commercial banks.

Project Status

Main structural works reached advanced stages by early 2026; foundations were completed in 2023. Construction is approximately 60% complete with a target handover by late 2026 or early 2027.

Research Notes

Project existence is confirmed by multiple authoritative sources (EU, Serbian Govt, CEB). The main financing risk is settled, transitioning the bank's interest toward equipment suppliers only.

Issues & Risks

The project has experienced significant cost overruns from €118M to €141M and historical tender delays (2017-2023). The urban site remains constrained due to its proximity to existing medical facilities.

Updates

2026-03: Construction in full swing as of March 2026; official government tours confirm project remains on track for late 2026 patient readiness.
