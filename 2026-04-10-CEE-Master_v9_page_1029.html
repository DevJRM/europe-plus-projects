Project Summary

Comprehensive modernization of the Kragujevac automotive plant for the serial production of the electric Fiat Grande Panda and the upcoming Citroen e-C3. This facility serves as the first dedicated Battery Electric Vehicle (BEV) production hub in the Western Balkans, supporting Stellantis' 'Dare Forward 2030' strategy.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

secured

Total EUR 190m investment fully funded: EUR 48m Serbian state subsidy, EUR 73m EIB direct loan (signed July 2024), and EUR 69m from Stellantis internal cash flow/corporate debt.

ECA/MLA Involvement

confirmed

EIB provided a EUR 73m direct loan in July 2024 to FCA Srbija under the 'Electric Vehicles Manufacturing Serbia' project window. No commercial guarantee structure utilized.

Project Status

Trial production commenced July 2024. Serial production of the Fiat Grande Panda (EV and hybrid) reached full capacity in early 2026 with the addition of a third shift. Expansion for Citroen e-C3 production announced March 2026.

Research Notes

Project successfully transitioned Kragujevac from ICE production (Fiat 500L) to BEV. The site is a key pillar of Serbia's export economy, expected to account for a material share of national manufacturing output.

Issues & Risks

Supply chain sensitivity regarding high-voltage components and battery cells sourced from the Stellantis global network; geopolitical risks affecting logistics via the Red Sea.

Updates

2026-03-19: Official announcement of the expansion of the Kragujevac facility to include production of the Citroen e-C3 EV models.
