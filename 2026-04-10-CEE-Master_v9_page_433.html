Project Summary

Transformation of the former KVV Liepājas Metalurgs steel plant site into a sustainable industrial hub focusing on green hydrogen production, Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF), and CO2 storage and transshipment infrastructure.

Sponsor

Liepāja Special Economic Zone Authority in collaboration with SIA NorSAF and CIS Liepāja

  • Liepāja Special Economic Zone Authority (LV)
  • CIS Liepāja (LV)
  • Avia Solutions Group (via NorSAF JV) (LT)
Guarantor

none

No direct sovereign debt guarantee. Designated as a 'Strategic Industrial Project', facilitating administrative support and access to EU funds via the Ministry of Economics.

Contracted Suppliers

No suppliers identified

Significant presence in Baltic grid stabilization and industrial electrification. Medium confidence: sector fit.

Project Scope

Green Hydrogen & SAF Production Plant €487.5m (75.0%)
Establishment of an industrial-scale facility for H2 and synthetic fuels with a 100,000t SAF annual capacity target.
Industrial Park Infrastructure Modernization €97.5m (15.0%)
Upgrading electrical grids, water supply, and remediation of the former industrial site.
CO2 Capture & Transshipment Terminal €65.0m (10.0%)
Infrastructure for the handling and storage of carbon for synthetic fuel synthesis.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

not yet secured

Actively seeking debt and equity partners. Eligible for the EU Modernisation Fund and Recovery and Resilience Facility (RRF). Commercial financing tranches for the main plant capex remain open for 2026-2027 closing.

ECA/MLA Involvement

eligible

Eligible for EIB/EBRD and NIB support under green industrial mandates. Potential for ECA-backed commercial lending if technology is sourced from Germany (Hermes) or US (EXIM). No direct MLA lending confirmed yet.

Project Status

The project is in the advanced planning and permitting stage. The Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) program for the NorSAF facility was finalized following public consultations in Q1 2026, moving the project toward shovel-ready status for 2027.

Research Notes

Project repositioned from 'Green Steel' to 'Green Industrial Hub'. Confirmed as a distinct entity from port dredging projects (LV-018). Source diversity optimized to utilize project-specific EIA and trade news.

Issues & Risks

EIA compliance for large-scale chemical processes; volatility of Baltic energy prices impacting green H2 margins; requirement for long-term SAF offtake agreements.

Updates

2026-03: Public consultation for the NorSAF hydrogen and SAF production facility EIA concluded in late March 2026, a vital milestone for 2027 shovel-ready status.
