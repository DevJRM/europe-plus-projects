Project Summary

National strategic programme for the development of the first High-Speed Rail (VRT) corridors and the modernization of the core rail network. Key components include the Prague-Brno-Ostrava and Prague-Dresden corridors, ETCS Level 2 signaling rollout across 4,800 km, and track electrification. The programme aims to modernize the Czech Republic's position in the TEN-T core network with construction on pilot sections beginning in 2026.

Sponsor

Guarantor

sovereign

Funding is provided via the State Fund for Transport Infrastructure (SFDI). While SFDI acts as the funding channel, the ultimate credit is the Czech Sovereign through the national budget.

Project Scope

High-Speed Rail (VRT) New Lines €10175.0m (55.0%)
Construction of dedicated high-speed tracks for 320 km/h (VRT 1, VRT 2, and VRT 4 corridors).
Core Network Modernisation & Electrification €5550.0m (30.0%)
Upgrading existing corridors to TEN-T standards, including substation upgrades.
ETCS L2 Signaling & Control €2775.0m (15.0%)
Implementation of European Train Control System Level 2 across the core network.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

partially secured

Financing is a mix of state budget (SFDI), EU CEF grants, and EIB direct loans (approx. EUR 3bn annual investment capacity). A significant financing gap of approx. EUR 5bn remains specifically for the 2026-2027 high-speed equipment and civil tranches, intended for PPP or ECA-backed commercial structures.

ECA/MLA Involvement

eligible

EIB is providing direct lending for core infrastructure. Commercial opportunity lies in ECA-backed financing (e.g., Euler Hermes, SACE) for high-speed rolling stock and specialized technology tranches where direct EU/state grants are not sufficient.

Project Status

The programme is transitioning from advanced planning to construction. Tendering for pilot VRT sections (Prague-Běchovice – Poříčany) and core modernization lots is active in early 2026. Physical construction on first pilot sections slated for mid-2026.

Research Notes

SŽ's 2026 budget is approx. CZK 72.2bn. The EUR 18.5bn capex is the estimated long-term programme total. Rolling stock is excluded from the main capex calculation to align with the bank's core infrastructure focus, though it remains an ECA-backed debt opportunity.

Issues & Risks

Tender procedural reforms intended to increase international competition; land acquisition challenges for new VRT tracks through sensitive environmental zones.

Updates

2026-03: Launch of major procurement reforms to attract foreign contractors and accelerate VRT pilot sections. First specialized HSR tenders active as of March 2026.
