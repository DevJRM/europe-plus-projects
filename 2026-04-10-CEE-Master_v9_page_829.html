Project Summary

Development of the largest natural gas project in the Romanian Black Sea, targeting the Domino and Pelican South fields with estimated recoverable resources of approximately 100 billion cubic meters. First gas expected in 2027.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

secured

Fully funded via corporate means. Romgaz secured a EUR 1.15B syndicated loan and issued a EUR 500M green bond in 2025; OMV Petrom utilizes internal cash flows. No funding gap for 2026-2027 capex.

ECA/MLA Involvement

none

Sponsors utilize corporate debt and internal cash; no direct MLA/ECA lending at project level. Italy's SACE may provide ancillary supplier-side cover for Saipem.

Project Status

Project sanctioned (FID) in June 2023. Drilling operations commenced in 2025 using the Transocean Barents rig. Platform fabrication and pipeline laying on track for 2027 first gas.

Research Notes

Project fully funded. Capex updated to EUR 4B per OMV Petrom Q4 2025 presentation. Confirmed suppliers updated with Saipem (EUR 1.6B) and Expro added. Maire Tecnimont removed due to lack of verified project-specific contract in 2026. Opportunity remains none as project is fully financed and under construction.

Issues & Risks

Black Sea security risks due to regional conflict; potential for environmental NGO litigation; deepwater technical execution risks; NATO security coverage uncertainty.

Updates

2026-03: Drilling ongoing with Transocean Barents rig; platform delivery on track for installation; pipeline microtunnel works advancing.
