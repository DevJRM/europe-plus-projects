Project Summary

Expansion and operation of a large-scale industrial facility for the recovery of strategic metals including lithium, cobalt, nickel, and platinum group metals from spent Li-ion batteries and automotive catalysts. The project aims to secure European critical raw material supply chains through a JV-operated facility (AE Elemental) and the large-scale 'Polvolt' expansion initiative.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

partially secured

Secured EUR 52M from EBRD (direct lending) and approximately EUR 150M in EU/Polish grants (Polvolt). A material financing gap of approx. EUR 50-100M remains for the final commercial tranches of refining equipment and scale-up capex for 2026-2027.

ECA/MLA Involvement

confirmed

EBRD provides direct lending (EUR 52M). Project is an IPCEI recipient (EU grants). No current ECA guarantee structure in place for commercial bank portion, though eligible for EU CRM de-risking instruments.

Project Status

Initial recycling phase opened June 2024. Currently constructing the high-value hydrometallurgical and lithium refining expansion (Polvolt project) with a ramp-up target through 2026-2027.

Research Notes

Project capex updated from initial EUR 120M to EUR 250M to reflect the broader 'Polvolt' expansion hubs in Zawiercie. EBRD link corrected to working path. IFC removed from immediate shareholders as current JV focus is with Ascend Elements.

Issues & Risks

Securing predictable volumes of black mass feedstock amidst evolving EU battery regulations; high electricity costs in Poland impacting refining margins.

Updates

2025-11-15: European Commission approved EUR 150M grant for the strategic Polvolt project to implement lithium refining in Zawiercie.
