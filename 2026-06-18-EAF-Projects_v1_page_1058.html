Project Summary

Development and construction of one of the largest onshore wind farms in the Czech Republic, located near the village of Pohledy in the Svitavy District. The project involves the installation of approximately 13-15 high-capacity wind turbines with a total installed capacity of 70-80 MW. As of early 2026, the project has secured environmental impact assessment (EIA) approval and is in the territorial and building permit phase, targeting construction start in 2027.

Project Scope

Construction of a new high-voltage substation, underground cabling, and connection to the ČEZ Distribuce grid.

Access roads, turbine foundations, crane pads, and site preparation in hilly terrain.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

not yet secured

Equity is provided by the J&T Group. The project is seeking approximately EUR 75-80 million in external project finance debt. Formal negotiations with commercial banks are expected to commence in H2 2026, following the receipt of the final building permit.

ECA/MLA Involvement

eligible

Project is eligible for European Investment Bank (EIB) financing under REPowerEU and other green lending facilities. It is also eligible for grants and loans from the Czech Modernisation Fund (administered by SFŽP), specifically the RES+ program for renewable energy sources.

Project Status

Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) approved (CENIA decision PAK876, late 2025). The project is currently in the territorial and building permit phase. Construction is targeted to begin in 2027, with commissioning expected in 2028.

Issues & Risks

1. Local community opposition remains a risk despite EIA approval, potentially causing delays during the permitting process. 2. Grid connection capacity in the Pardubice region is constrained, requiring confirmation from ČEZ Distribuce and potential network reinforcement costs. 3. Project timing is dependent on receipt of the final building permit, which is a key condition precedent for financial close.
