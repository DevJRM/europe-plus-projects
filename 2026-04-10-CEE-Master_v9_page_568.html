Project Summary

An integrated green hydrogen facility in Northern Poland (Pomeranian Voivodeship) featuring 400 MW of wind and 800 MW of solar capacity. The project is designed to produce green hydrogen and waste heat equivalent to 2.2 million MWh annually for industrial use and municipal heating in the Tricity area.

Project Status

The project is in the planning and permitting phase. While initial environmental work began in 2023, public evidence of progression toward construction has been limited in early 2026, coinciding with the sponsor's strategic priority shift toward BESS projects.

Research Notes

Distinct from Polenergia's H2Silesia. The sponsor, Green Capital S.A., has publicly shifted its immediate focus to BESS projects as of 2026-Q1, which may further delay this hydrogen initiative. Project existence verified via developer portfolio and industry reports.

Issues & Risks

Key risks include grid connection congestion in the Gdynia/Gdańsk node and regulatory uncertainty regarding the permitting of large-scale hydrogen storage for off-grid scenarios.

Updates

2026-01-12: Project was not included in the latest round of Polish government/BGK hydrogen subsidies, maintaining its unfunded status.
