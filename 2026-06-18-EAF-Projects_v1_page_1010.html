Project Summary

Toyota is investing EUR 680 million to expand its Kolín plant, transitioning it into a hub for Battery Electric Vehicle (BEV) production. The project includes a new dedicated battery pack assembly facility, a new paint shop, and advanced welding lines. The plant's footprint is expanding from 152,000 m2 to 173,000 m2 to accommodate a new urban BEV model for the European market, with production expected to commence in 2026. This follows the start of Aygo X hybrid production in late 2025.

Project Scope

Battery assembly facility and system integration €238.0m (35.0%)
Construction of a dedicated facility for high-voltage battery pack assembly and integration into the BEV platform.
New Paint and Welding Shops €204.0m (30.0%)
Installation of high-efficiency automated paint lines and specialized BEV chassis welding robots.
Final Assembly Line Retooling €170.0m (25.0%)
Modifications to existing lines to support parallel production of ICE-hybrids and full BEVs.
Facility Expansion and Logistics €68.0m (10.0%)
Expansion of total plant area by 21,000 m2 and logistics hub upgrades.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

secured

Fully funded through Toyota's internal corporate cash flows and regional incentives. No external commercial bank debt or project finance syndicate has been requested or announced. The sponsor's AA+/Aa3 credit profile allows for self-financing of the EUR 680M capex.

ECA/MLA Involvement

none

While German and Japanese equipment content would make the project 'eligible' for Euler Hermes or NEXI support, Toyota's self-funding strategy renders ECA/MLA involvement unnecessary for this transaction.

Project Status

Retooling and expansion started post-September 2025 announcement. As of April 2026, equipment installation for the battery assembly line and paint shop is in advanced stages. Hybrid production is operational on parallel lines while BEV commissioning proceeds toward H2 2026 launch.

Research Notes

Capex updated from EUR 400M to EUR 680M based on Sep 2025 corporate announcements. Financing confirmed as purely internal.

Issues & Risks

Main risks include softening European demand for BEVs, potential trade tensions regarding local content requirements, and the operational complexity of parallel producing ICE-hybrids and BEVs on a compact site.

Updates

2026-02: CzechInvest confirms the TMMCZ expansion as one of the largest ongoing manufacturing investments in the country, with construction of the battery facility on schedule for a 2026 SOP.
