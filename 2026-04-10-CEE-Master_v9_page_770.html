Project Summary

Construction of a 400 MW / 800 MWh standalone battery energy storage system (BESS) at the Dolna Odra Power Plant site in Gryfino. The project is designed to provide grid stability for the Western Poland industrial corridor and is a key part of PGE's strategy to integrate renewables. The project secured a 17-year capacity market contract starting in 2029.

The main project capex remains unfunded as of April 2026. PGE plans to utilize a combination of corporate debt, potential green bonds, and is seeking grants from the Modernisation Fund and EU-related recovery mechanisms.

While BESS assets are generally eligible for EIB REPowerEU framework support, there is no confirmed project-specific allocation or signed guarantee for the Gryfino tranche as of April 2026.

Project Status

As of April 10, 2026, the project is in the final contract negotiation phase following the selection of the EPC consortium in March 2026. Construction is expected to commence following Financial Close, with a commercial operation date (COD) target of 2029.

Research Notes

Project verified as a standalone 400MW BESS. Previous [SUSPICIOUS] flag regarding a 150MWp solar hybrid component was resolved; all current procurement documentation and PGE announcements focusing strictly on standalone storage to meet capacity market obligations.

Issues & Risks

Grid interconnection capacity at the Dolna Odra coal node; supply chain risks for lithium-ion battery cells; finalization of Polish regulatory framework for energy storage tariffs.

Updates

2026-03: Consortium of Spec Bau Polska and El Professional selected as the preferred bidder for EPC works with a bid of approximately PLN 1.14 billion.
