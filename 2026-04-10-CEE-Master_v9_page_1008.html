Project Summary

Construction of a series of 10 run-of-river hydropower plants on the Ibar River between Raška and Kraljevo with a total capacity of 103-120 MW. The project is a strategic greenfield initiative by state utility EPS and Italian partners to utilize the hydro potential of Central Serbia, targeting an annual production of 420-450 GWh.

Project Scope

10 Run-of-River Dams and Powerhouses €199.5m (70.0%)
Construction of low-head dams and installation of Kaplan turbines across 10 sites.
Grid Connection Infrastructure €42.8m (15.0%)
Transmission lines and substations to connect the cascade to the national grid.
Environmental Mitigation €42.8m (15.0%)
Fish ladders and riverbed protection works to meet E&S standards.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

not yet secured

Financing is not yet obtained. The involvement of Italian partner Seci Energia makes the equipment tranches eligible for SACE (Italian ECA) buyer credit cover, which is the primary expected path for commercial bank involvement.

ECA/MLA Involvement

eligible

SACE (Italian ECA) is highly eligible to provide cover for the Italian equipment tranche via Seci Energia. EBRD is eligible for credit enhancement/guarantees under green transition frameworks for Serbia.

Project Status

The project is in the pre-FID stage. Following a 2023 tender for technical documentation, it was reaffirmed in the 2025 energy strategy amendments. Environmental and spatial planning is ongoing as of 2026.

Research Notes

Project is a greenfield new-build cascade, distinctly different from the rehabilitation works at Djerdap or Vlasina. Financing closing realistically 2027.

Issues & Risks

Persistent environmental opposition from local NGOs and activists (e.g., 'Defend the Rivers of Mt. Stara Planina' branches) regarding biodiversity in the Ibar valley; potential for Bern Convention scrutiny.

Updates

2025-03: Serbian Ministry of Mining and Energy included the Ibar cascade in the official 2025 amendments to the Strategic Energy Development Plan until 2030.
