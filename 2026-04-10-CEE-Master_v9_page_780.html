Project Summary

BC-Wind is a 390 MW bottom-fixed offshore wind farm in the Polish Baltic Sea, located approximately 23 km off the coast of Choczewo and Krokowa. Developed by Ocean Winds (a 50/50 joint venture between ENGIE and EDP Renewables) through the SPV C-WIND POLSKA SP ZOO, the project utilizes 26 Siemens Gamesa 14.7 MW turbines. It is a critical component of Poland's energy transition, aiming to supply clean energy to approximately 500,000 households. The project secured a Contract for Difference (CfD) and reached financial close in late 2025.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

secured

Financial close was achieved on December 1, 2025, for a total of approximately EUR 2 billion. The package includes a EUR 600 million direct loan from the European Investment Bank (EIB), with the balance provided by a syndicate of 13 commercial banks (including CA CIB, Santander, and BNP Paribas) and ICO. All main project funding for Phase I (390 MW) is now in place.

ECA/MLA Involvement

confirmed

Confirmed institutional involvement: European Investment Bank (EIB) provided EUR 600 million direct loan. ICO (Spain) also participated in the syndicate. No active ECA cover (e.g., Euler Hermes, EKF) was finalized in the public close data, but commercial lenders participate alongside the DFI tranche.

Project Status

Final Investment Decision (FID) was reached in December 2025 following financial close. Onshore construction works for the substation commenced in Q1 2026. Offshore installation is scheduled to begin in 2027, with full commercial operations expected by 2028.

Research Notes

Project size corrected from 800MW (original planning) to 390MW (active financing package). Financing and status updated to reflect successful Dec 2025 close.

Issues & Risks

Supply chain tightness for offshore installation vessels; grid synchronization risks with the Polish TSO (PSE); cost volatility in raw materials for subsea cables.

Updates
