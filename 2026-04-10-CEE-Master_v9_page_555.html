Project Summary

Construction of a new water level structure (barrage) on the Vistula River below Włocławek, including a run-of-river hydroelectric power plant (up to 80MW), a navigation lock for Class IV waterway standards, and a fish pass. The project aims to provide flood protection, ensure the stability of the existing Włocławek dam, and improve inland navigation.

Financing Status

not yet secured

Main construction capex (estimated at 9bn PLN / ~2.1bn EUR) remains unfunded. Only preliminary documentation and pre-investment studies have received budget allocations. No commercial debt or EIB facilities have been formally signed as of April 2026.

ECA/MLA Involvement

eligible

Eligible for EIB financing under European Green Deal/Climate Adaptation frameworks. However, no active project appraisal is listed in EIB databases due to environmental permit revocation.

Project Status

The project is currently in a legal and administrative stalemate. The environmental decision was revoked by the General Director for Environmental Protection (GDOŚ) in late 2024/early 2025. As of early 2026, the government is considering special legislation (specustawa) to bypass administrative hurdles, but technical design and main tenders remain pending.

Research Notes

Project identity corrected from Oder-Vistula Program prefix to Stopień Wodny Siarzewo. Capex updated to reflect 2025-2026 estimates of 9bn PLN. Date updated to April 08, 2026.

Issues & Risks

Significant environmental opposition and legal challenges regarding Natura 2000 sites, river connectivity, and fish migration. The environmental permit was revoked in early 2025, halting progress.

Updates

2026-02: Parliamentary discussions and local government appeals for special legislation (specustawa) to restart the investment following the 2025 permit revocation.
