Project Summary

Construction of the central maintenance, storage, and operational hub for the Belgrade Metro system. The Makiš Depot includes the primary maintenance facility, the Operational Control Center (OCC) for automated train supervision, and a 72-hectare plateau for fleet storage. It is the critical operational prerequisite for the commissioning of Line 1.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

partially secured

Initial civil works funded via Chinese bilateral loans (approx. EUR 178M). Systems and rolling stock partially covered by French Bpifrance-backed buyer credits (approx. EUR 530M). Material financing gaps remain for supplemental tech integration and OCC IT infrastructure estimated at EUR 100M+.

ECA/MLA Involvement

confirmed

Bpifrance (France) provides buyer credit guarantees for the French tranche; Sinosure (China) covers the Chinese civil works tranche. Funding for the remaining tech gap is eligible for similar ECA-wrapped commercial bank structures.

Project Status

Plateau works are approximately 80% complete as of March 2026. Following the Alstom turnkey contract signing on March 27, 2026, facility construction and systems integration are scheduled to ramp up in Q2 2026.

Research Notes

The Makiš Depot is a distinct procurement package within Line 1. While core contracts were signed in March 2026, the specific financing for the final systems integration phase remains a target for commercial bank syndication under ECA cover.

Issues & Risks

Geotechnical challenges involving high water tables at Makiško Polje requiring extensive soil stabilization; environmental opposition regarding the proximity to Belgrade's primary water protection zone.

Updates

2026-03: Turnkey contract for Line 1 equipment and depot systems signed with Alstom on March 27, 2026, marking the move to full-scale facility execution.
