Project Summary

An integrated national strategic investment led by the Poznań Supercomputing and Networking Center (PSNC) to establish a high-performance computing (HPC) and quantum computing ecosystem. The infrastructure includes the operational 'PIAST-Q' (EuroQCS-Poland) quantum system and the 'PIAST-AI Factory' launching in Q3 2026, designed to support secure computing for healthcare, energy, and digital transition sectors within the EuroHPC JU network.

Project Scope

Quantum Hardware (PIAST-Q) €20.0m (20.0%)
Installation of an AQT-supplied trapped-ion quantum computer hosted at PSNC as part of EuroHPC JU.
AI Infrastructure (PIAST-AI) €60.0m (60.0%)
Deployment of high-density GPU acceleration clusters for large-scale AI model training and services.
Facility & Quantum Connectivity €20.0m (20.0%)
Specialized cooling units and integration with the PIONIER-Q Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) network.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

partially secured

Quantum hardware costs (approx EUR 16-20M) are 50% funded by EuroHPC JU. AI Factory initial phase is funded by a 340M PLN state grant. A financing gap remains for the 2026-2027 commercial scale-up and additional GPU capacity.

ECA/MLA Involvement

eligible

Eligible for EIB 'Innovation and Digital' window and InvestEU guarantees. EuroHPC JU provides direct grant funding but does not currently exclude commercial lending for local infrastructure gaps.

Project Status

The quantum computer (PIAST-Q) was inaugurated in June 2025 and is operational. The AI Factory (PIAST-AI) infrastructure is currently being installed with a service launch targeted for Q3 2026. Further scale-up phases are planned for 2027.

Research Notes

Project name adjusted to reflect PIAST-Q and PIAST-AI components. Capex of EUR 215M was found to be speculative; revised to EUR 100M for verified scope. Lumi-Q removed as it is hosted in Ostrava, CZ.

Issues & Risks

Global supply chain constraints for latest H100/B200 GPU series impacting AI Factory delivery timelines; complexities in cross-border QKD network interoperability.

Updates

2026-03-24: Formal launch of the pioneer Polish QKD network segment at PSNC, marking completion of the quantum cybersecurity layer.
