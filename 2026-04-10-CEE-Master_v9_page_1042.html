Project Summary

Long-standing 102 MW onshore wind farm project in pre-construction, planned with 17 turbines of 6 MW each. Originally permitted in 2012, the project is a 50/50 joint venture between NIS and MET Group via the SPV Wind Park Plandište d.o.o., aimed at Serbia's renewable diversification under the national 500 MW wind quota. As of late 2025, the Serbian government has expressed interest in a potential takeover to accelerate development after historical delays.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

not yet secured

Unfunded. Project requires approx. EUR 110M in debt. Previous plans for NIS/MET commercial tendering are stalled pending the outcome of government takeover discussions.

ECA/MLA Involvement

none

No institutional commitment found. While theoretically eligible for EBRD 'Green Economy' frameworks, lead sponsor ownership (Gazprom Neft) currently restricts participation from major Western MLAs/ECAs.

Project Status

Pre-construction phase. Historical delays since 2012; Jul 2025 reports indicate potential government takeover to bypass inertia. Project remains in the pre-FID stage as of April 2026.

Research Notes

Project is one of the oldest in the Serbian pipeline. Key monitorable is the transition from NIS/MET ownership to a potential state-led vehicle which would change the credit profile to Sovereign.

Issues & Risks

Geopolitical sensitivities regarding Gazprom Neft (53% NIS owner); historical project inertia and permitting expiration; uncertainty regarding the Jul 2025 government takeover proposal.

Updates

2025-07: Government of Serbia announces interest in taking over the project from NIS to ensure completion by 2027.
