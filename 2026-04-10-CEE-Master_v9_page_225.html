Project Summary

Replacement of existing blast furnaces with two hybrid Electric Arc Furnaces (EAF) to reduce CO2 emissions by more than 80%. This is a critical 'Green Steel' project for the Czech heavy industry sector, currently subject to restructuring due to the insolvency proceedings of Liberty Ostrava a.s., which halted primary production in 2024. The Czech government views this as a strategic decarbonization project and is seeking new investors to continue the transformation.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

not yet secured

A CZK 8.9 billion (approx EUR 350M) grant was conditionally approved by the Czech Modernisation Fund (SFZP) but is frozen pending resolution of the sponsor's insolvency and the entry of a new credible investor. No debt financing is in place. The total project cost is estimated at EUR 350M, potentially fully covered by the grant, leaving no clear debt tranche for a commercial bank under the current structure.

ECA/MLA Involvement

eligible

SACE (Italy) would be the natural ECA for the Danieli contract. However, ECA cover is currently unavailable due to the sponsor's insolvency status. Eligibility would be restored for a new, creditworthy project sponsor.

Project Status

Project is on hold pending restructuring and new investor search. Technology contract with Danieli signed but paused due to sponsor insolvency. Environmental permitting (EIA) was advanced but not finalized. Groundbreaking timeline uncertain until new ownership structure is resolved.

Research Notes

Project remains a strategic Czech industrial decarbonization priority but is stalled. Distinct from CZ-049 (Chomutov). Bank's opportunity is contingent on successful restructuring and creation of a viable debt financing need.

Issues & Risks

1) Sponsor insolvency (Liberty Ostrava a.s. is in judicial reorganization). 2) Uncertain ownership and financial structure for project continuation. 3) Grant financing (CZK 8.9B) may cover full capex, leaving no debt requirement. 4) High energy price volatility impacts EAF operational economics.

Updates

2026-02: Czech government continues talks with potential investors to salvage the Ostrava steel plant and its decarbonization project. Modernisation Fund grant remains conditionally approved but inactive.
