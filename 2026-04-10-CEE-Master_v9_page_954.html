Project Summary

Suspended development of an industrial complex designed to process jadarite ore into battery-grade lithium carbonate (27,000 tpa), boric acid, and sodium sulfate. The project is a key pillar of the EU-Serbia strategic partnership on sustainable raw materials, aimed at securing European battery supply chains.

Financing Status

Initial US$2.4B internal capital commitment from 2021 remains on hold. No active commercial or ECA-backed debt raising is currently underway due to the project's suspended status.

ECA/MLA Involvement

eligible

The project is eligible for support under the EU Critical Raw Materials Act; however, no specific ECA (e.g., Euler Hermes) or MLA (e.g., EBRD) commitment has been finalized as development is currently stalled.

Project Status

Project officially under 'care and maintenance' since November 2025. While the Serbian government reinstated the spatial plan in July 2024 and the EU granted Strategic Project status in June 2025, active development is halted pending the resolution of an investment treaty arbitration initiated by Rio Tinto in January 2026.

Research Notes

Project isolated to the processing facility. Total $2.4B capex includes the mine. 50% allocation to processing is a market estimate for the chemical complex component. Care and maintenance status confirmed April 2026.

Issues & Risks

Severe social and environmental opposition resulting in 2025-2026 protests; €1.5B investment arbitration ongoing between Rio Tinto and the Republic of Serbia.

Updates

2026-01: Rio Tinto initiated legal proceedings/arbitration against the Serbian government following the halt in project development permits.
