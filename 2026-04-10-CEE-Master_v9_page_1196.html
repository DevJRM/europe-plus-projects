Project Summary

Expansion of the existing Batajnica intermodal terminal to double container throughput capacity to approx. 400,000 TEU, including 4 hectares of new storage areas, specialized rail gantry crane tracks, and a Terminal Operating System (TOS) for digital customs integration.

Sponsor

Ministry of Construction, Transport and Infrastructure

  • Government of Serbia (RS)
Guarantor

sovereign

State-backed infrastructure under the Ministry of Construction; eligible for EU IPA matching grants which support sovereign borrowing capacity.

Potential Supply Opportunities

Major provider of RMG cranes for European terminals; active in Serbian industrial sector (e.g. Elixir Group waste-to-energy project 2024), providing a strong Finland/Finnvera ECA angle.

Established presence in Serbian transport infrastructure (rail and road) and capable of heavy-duty pavement works required for container yards.

Global provider of Terminal Operating Systems (TOS) used in dry ports and intermodal hubs to manage customs and throughput efficiency.

Project Scope

Container Yard Expansion €33.0m (60.0%)
Additional 4 hectares of reinforced concrete pavement for container stacking and maneuvering.
Gantry Cranes & Equipment €16.5m (30.0%)
Procurement of automated rail-mounted gantry cranes (RMG) for increased throughput.
IT & Logistics Software €5.5m (10.0%)
Implementation of a Terminal Operating System (TOS) and digital customs interface.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

partially secured

Land and initial studies state-funded; material financing gap of approx. EUR 40M remains for specialized equipment and civil works, planned for 2027 start.

ECA/MLA Involvement

eligible

Eligible for WBIF (Western Balkans Investment Framework) grants and EBRD sovereign-backed loans. Technical Assistance already provided via WBIF for Phase 2 preparation.

Project Status

Phase 1 is operational; Phase 2 feasibility and technical assistance for expansion were accelerated in early 2026 to meet rising demand from the Belgrade-Budapest railway corridor.

Research Notes

The terminal acts as a 'dry port' for the Belgrade region and is the first multimodal hub of its kind in Serbia, intended to serve as a model for regional logistics development.

Issues & Risks

Integration with the high-speed rail traffic schedules on the Belgrade-Budapest line; noise mitigation for nearby residential zones in the Batajnica district.

Updates

2026-03: Phase 2 planning accelerated following traffic reports showing Phase 1 handling volumes significantly above initial 2024 projections.
