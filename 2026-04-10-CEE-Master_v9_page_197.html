Project Summary

Construction of the missing Central Bohemian sections of the D3 motorway (sections 0301–0305, approx. 60km total) connecting Prague to the South Bohemian region and the Austrian border. The project is a critical part of the Trans-European Transport Network (TEN-T).

Financial Structure

Financing Status

not yet secured

UNFUNDED for major capex. While preparatory works and land acquisition are state-funded, the main construction capex (approx. EUR 2.4bn) represents a significant financing gap intended to be covered by EIB framework loans and potentially commercial lending tranches in 2027-2028. No commercial debt has been closed as of April 2026.

ECA/MLA Involvement

eligible

Eligible for EIB sovereign-backed framework loans. While EIB is active in CZ road infrastructure (e.g., Prague Ring road), no specific allocation or direct lending for D3 Central Bohemian sections is confirmed yet. The structure may allow for EIB-guaranteed commercial tranches.

Project Status

The project is in advanced planning (DSP documentation phase). Geotechnical surveys for tunnels commenced in Q1 2026. Main construction tenders for the first major packages are expected in late 2026 or 2027.

Research Notes

CAPEX was corrected to reflect the full estimated cost of the Central Bohemian sections (CZK 60-70B converted to EUR 2.4B), rather than just initial study packages. Original value of 480 was identified as an internal inconsistency during Stage 1 audit.

Issues & Risks

Residual environmental litigation from local NGOs regarding the corridor through the Posázaví protected area. Although court rulings in 2024-2025 were favorable to the project, delays in building permits remain a risk.

Updates

2026-03: Ministry of Transport confirmed D3 Central Bohemian sections as the top strategic road priority for the 2026-2030 cycle. Geotechnical drilling for the tunnel sections began in March 2026.
