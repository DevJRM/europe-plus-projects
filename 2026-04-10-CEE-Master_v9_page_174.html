Project Summary

Development of a new high-tech greenfield manufacturing facility for heavy-duty corrugated cardboard and specialized industrial packaging. The plant is located in the Trakia Economic Zone and primarily targets the automotive and electronics sectors in Southeast Europe, utilizing advanced automation and sustainable production processes.

Sponsor

Tri-Wall Bulgaria EOOD

  • Tri-Wall Limited (HK)
  • Rengo Co., Ltd. (JP)
Guarantor

corporate

The project is expected to be backed by the balance sheet of the parent company Tri-Wall Limited (HK), with ultimate support from the Tokyo-listed Rengo Co., Ltd.

No suppliers identified

BHS is a preferred global technology partner for Tri-Wall; however, a specific contract award for the Bulgarian plant has not been publicly documented as of April 2026.
Bobst (CH)

Strong affiliation with Rengo (Japanese parent) and significant market share in high-capacity industrial corrugators.

Project Scope

Corrugated Board Production Line €23.1m (55.0%)
High-speed automated corrugator for heavy-duty grades and industrial packaging.
Automated Converting & Printing €10.5m (25.0%)
Robotic die-cutting and multi-color flexo printing lines for specialized orders.
Logistics & Smart Warehousing €8.4m (20.0%)
Automated inventory management and finished goods distribution hub.

Financial Structure

Financing Status

partially secured

Initial construction phase is equity-funded by the Tri-Wall Group. The sponsor is reportedly exploring commercial debt tranches to finance specific high-tech equipment installations for the second half of 2026.

ECA/MLA Involvement

none

No official MLA or ECA involvement confirmed. The project is eligible for ECA cover from Euler Hermes (Germany) or SERV (Switzerland) depending on final equipment selections.

Project Status

Groundbreaking ceremony held on January 28, 2026, with the Minister of Innovation. Construction is currently in the early stages (foundation and site works) with production scheduled to commence in late 2026/early 2027.

Research Notes

The project represents a strategic shift of capacity for Tri-Wall from Turkey to Bulgaria to secure EU-based production for the European automotive supply chain. While existence is highly verified, capex figures in media range from EUR 30M to 42M.

Issues & Risks

Rising prices of raw paper materials and regional logistics bottlenecks in the Plovdiv industrial corridor.

Updates

2026-02: Announcement of increased investment scope to over EUR 40M to include additional automation for electronics packaging.
